2. Materials

Fiber

  • Natural (from plant or animal): cotton, wool, silk, linen
  • Synthetic: polyester, nylon

Yarn: made of fiber

Fabric: made from yarn

  • Woven: interlacing two sets of yarn at right angles, for structured, jackets, pants

    • Denim: sturdy cotton twill, used for jeans and jackets
    • Satin: weaving silk or polyester in a particular way to create smooth, glossy surface

  • Knitted: interlocking loops of yarn, more stretch, for athletic, minimal design

    • Jersey: soft, stretch knit
    • Fleece: developed from polyester

3. Production Technique

Cut and sew: transform fabric into clothing

  • Pattern making: create pattern act as blueprint, made from paper and outline different components, like sleeves, bodices, pant legs
  • Cutting: fabric is cut according to the pattern, manually with scissors
  • Sewing: sew pieces together

Dyeing: add color

  • Batik: manual dyeing using wax to cover parts that will not be dyed
  • Tie-dyeing: tied in pattern and dyed
  • Chemical dyeing: using synthetic dyes for more controlled and uniform application

Printing: adding pattern or images to fabric

  • Block printign
  • Screen printing

Finishing: enhance appearance and performance

  • Bleaching: lighten or remove fabrics’s original color
  • Shrinking: Heat or steam to ensure fabric does not shrink after first wash
